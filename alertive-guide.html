Introduction

The Alertive mobile app connects healthcare professionals at the point of care with relevant colleagues and all the information they need to make better decisions and improve patient outcomes. It empowers healthcare providers to deliver higher-quality care with more effective, efficient and motivated staff.

Any Device

Fully secure, bring-your-own-device compliant messaging on any device — lowering cost versus proprietary hardware and enabling rollout across the whole organisation.

Interoperability

Works alongside other hospital systems, including telephony, electronic patient records and pathology — reducing errors and improving the audit trail.

Rapid Feedback

Directory, roles and teams help you reach the right person first time, with delivery confirmations and response status giving instant feedback.

Flexible Communication

From lock-screen-breaking critical alerts to patient-context chats and shared tasks, with video, voice and text attachments throughout.

02

Getting Started

Installation

Search Alertive in the App Store (Apple) or Google Play Store (Android) and install the app. You'll then move through onboarding screens — read each one and tap Continue to proceed.

Notifications

When prompted, select Allow for critical notifications. This is how Alertive notifies you of an emergency, and the app can't work properly without this permission.

⚠ Critical alerts prompt

"Critical Alerts always play a sound and appear on the Lock Screen even if your iPhone is muted or Do Not Disturb is on. Manage Critical Alerts in Settings." Tap Allow.

Security

Choose a 4-digit Alertive passcode, different from any other passcode you use, and confirm it. On a personal or single-user device, you can also enable biometrics (fingerprint or face recognition) for faster, secure sign-in — toggle this any time in Settings.

Organisation ID & login

Enter the Organisation ID provided by your Trust, then sign in with your Trust email and password. On a personal or single-user device, enable fast sign in to get in and out using your PIN or biometrics instead.

Every time you sign in, you land on your Accounts screen — your own inbox, plus any roles you hold or have access to.

03

Starting Your Shift

Fast sign in

On a personal or single-user device, fast sign in lets you get in and out of Alertive with a PIN or biometrics rather than re-entering full credentials each time. You can enable or disable this at any point in Settings. Even with fast sign in on, you can always fully sign out — this prompts the next user for their Organisation ID, Trust email and password.

Your account

Once signed in, tap your name on the Accounts screen to open your personal inbox — every conversation sent to you or started by you. Below that you'll find every role available to you or recently used.

Good habit

Acquire any role you're fulfilling at the start of your shift, so colleagues can reach you easily — then hand it over at the end of your working day.

Signing out

Use the sign-out icon at the top of Accounts. If you're occupying any roles, you'll be asked whether to keep them or relinquish them so they're empty for the next person. Signing out returns you to the fast sign in screen (or the Organisation ID screen if fast sign in is off) and you'll stop receiving notifications.

04

Handovers & Roles

What is a role?

Alongside messages sent to your personal account, Alertive lets you converse on behalf of a role — a responsibility or bleep replacement (e.g. Emergency Department Consultant) that's shared between whoever is on duty. Only one user can occupy a role at a time, and the app gives you several ways to pass it on.

Occupy or acquire

  1. Open AccountsGo to your accounts screen where all roles are listed.

  2. Select the roleiOS: swipe right to left on the role. Android: tap the three dots and choose Acquire. You can also long-press the role name, or tap it to open the contact card and select Acquire.

  3. Start sendingYou now occupy the role and can send conversations from it rather than your personal account.

Role status
Occupied — shown in purple with the current holder's name. Messages will be received.
Empty — shown in grey. Avoid sending alerts, tasks or chats here until someone acquires it.

Your contact directory shows occupancy for every role, so you can check before you message.

Relinquish, give & take

Always relinquish your role at the end of a shift, or give it directly to the colleague taking over.

Relinquish

iOS: swipe right to left on the role and choose Relinquish. Android: three dots → Relinquish. The role becomes Empty.

Give / Take

Give hands the role to a named colleague for their shift. Take transfers an occupied role to you and notifies the previous holder.

05

Find Who You Need

Contact directory

Tap the avatar icon in the bottom right of your Accounts screen to open the contact directory. Filter by Users, Roles or Groups using the tabs, or browse Favourites, Recents and All Contacts.

Users, roles & groups

Users are colleagues' direct accounts. Roles represent shift responsibilities and show purple with a name when occupied, grey when empty.

Groups are a saved collection of users and/or roles you can message together. Note: these aren't group broadcasts — unless a thread already exists, adding a group starts a new conversation with each member.

06

Alerts

Alerts let you get a colleague's attention promptly — a modern replacement for the traditional bleep. Configurations and naming vary by Trust, but alerts generally share these features:

Recipients & responses

Choose who receives the alert and what response options they're given. Once sent, track who has received it, read it and how they responded.

Rich context

Add a title and overview, and optionally attach patient context, images and audio.

Emergency High Medium Low
Emergency alerts

Recipients get a loud alert that breaks through the lock screen and can read out the emergency type. Non-urgent alerts deliver more quietly but still carry time sensitivity.

Switchboard users have dedicated, Trust-tailored tools for raising emergencies quickly, covered in separate training.

07

Tasks

Tasks let you exchange more in-depth, usually less time-critical information than an alert — so a colleague can act on exactly what you need, whether that's referring a patient, requesting a discharge summary or submitting an X-ray request.

Response flow

Unless accepted or declined outright, a task can move through stages of completion — e.g. In Progress or Blocked.

Rich detail

Add a title, description, type subcategory, priority label, due date, and attributes like location or patient details.

Expired, no-longer-relevant tasks appear with a strike-through — you can delete these from your work list whenever you like.

08

Conversation With Colleagues

Use Alertive for everyday conversations with colleagues instead of consumer apps like WhatsApp — keeping work and personal messaging separate, and letting you share patient details securely.

Starting a conversation

Tap the create button to start a direct or group conversation. Group conversations prompt you for a title (e.g. "Bed Management Coordinators") and can be built around a patient, with patient details attached manually or via an EPR integration. You can also start a conversation directly from a colleague's contact card, whether from the directory or an existing thread.

Once underway, exchange messages, voice notes, video and attachments depending on your settings.

Direct conversation

An ongoing conversation with a specific set of users.

Patient conversation

An integrated conversation tied to a specific patient's context.

09

Managing Priorities & Work

Colours and icons against conversations, tasks and alerts let you see what's in your inbox at a glance — including a blue dot for unread or unresponded items.

Filters & favourites

Use the tabs at the top of your inbox to narrow your list to a category, and the filter icon in the tab bar for a more specific view — tap once to switch it on, then tap "Filtered by" to change it. Pin important conversations to the top of your list, or flag items you need to revisit.

Search, pin or flag

Pull down on the screen at any time to search. To act on several items at once, tap Select to multi-select, then mark as read, unread, or delete.

Settings

Customise your settings

Tailor Alertive to your preferences — job title, contact number, login preferences and more. What's available depends on your organisation's configuration.

Provide feedback

Share your thoughts to help shape Alertive's future development directly from the Settings area.

Invite colleagues

Refer colleagues so they get full access to Alertive's communication features, streamlining collaboration across your team.

Access help & support

Reach training documents and the support team any time you need a hand.
